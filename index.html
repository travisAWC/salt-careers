The Salt Standards

Six principles that define how we think, build, and lead.

These aren't posters on a wall. They're practiced on every site, every day. If these resonate with you, you'll feel at home here.

Standard 01

Truth Over Tenure

Weight the Argument, Not the Title

A good idea from an apprentice beats a bad idea from the boss. We ask junior team members to speak first. Hierarchy is for logistics, not logic.

Standard 02

Disagree and Commit

Debate in the Studio, Unity on Site

At the toolbox meeting, we argue like lawyers. Once we're on the tools, we march like a battalion. If we picked Plan B, you become Plan B's biggest champion.

Standard 03

Lead with Questions

Coach > Command

Instead of "fix this," we ask "how would you approach solving this?" Telling creates followers. Asking creates leaders. Our 4:1 ask ratio is deliberate.

Standard 04

The Standard You Walk Past

No Excuses, Just Fixes

Ignoring a mistake is the same as approving it. We trade excuses for ownership. Everyone on a Salt site is a quality control officer.

Standard 05

Don't Just Deliver, Delight

The Swan Effect

Meeting the contract is baseline. We look for the extra 5%. Above the surface: calm, smiling, adaptive. Below: paddling furiously. The client never feels the splash.

Standard 06

Relentless Craftsmanship

The Homeowner Test

Craftsmanship means the work is a personal reflection of the maker. We ask: "If I were paying for this out of my own pocket, for my own house, would I accept it?"
